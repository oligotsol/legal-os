## Modifiers

### Rush turnaround

| Speed | Premium | Floor |
|---|---|---|
| Standard (72 hours from intake submission) | $0 | $0 |
| Rush (under 48 hours) | +$1,000 | +$500 |
| Emergency (under 24 hours) | +$2,000 | +$1,000 |

Quote rush ONLY when the client signals real urgency (court date, surgery scheduled,
deal closing, travel). Don't lead with rush.

### Out-of-state property

Per additional deed for a property in a state other than the client's primary state:
+$500 (floor $350). Each property is a separate deed.

### Tiered package discount (cross-service, applies to engagements $3,000+)

Highest tier only. Does NOT stack with floor pricing. Apply only when the engagement
value naturally hits the threshold without contortion.

## Pricing FAQ — what to say when asked

**"Why so expensive compared to LegalZoom / Rocket Lawyer / DIY?"**

> Those are document-generation products, not legal services — they hand you a template
> and a help center. We draft the document custom to your situation, file it correctly,
> and the work product is signed by an attorney. The flat fee covers the legal review
> and the right document for your facts, not just the form.

**"Why not hourly?"**

> Hourly bills surprise people. Flat fee means you know the number before you sign,
> and the only way it changes is if you ask for additional work outside the original
> scope. Most clients prefer the predictability.

**"Can I pay in installments?"**

> The full fee is due at engagement so we can begin drafting immediately. If a payment
> plan is necessary because of the engagement size (>$5,000), I can route that to
> Garrison for review — but the standard is paid at signing.

**"What if I want changes after the first draft?"**

> One round of revisions is included in every engagement — that's where we read your
> redlines or notes and incorporate them. A second round is standard if the changes
> are scope-fit. If the changes expand scope (new documents, new structures), that's a
> new engagement at the appropriate flat fee.

**"What if I cancel after I pay?"**

> Per the engagement letter: in TX and IA, your fee is in IOLTA trust and earned in
> milestones — one-third on signature, one-third on first-draft delivery, one-third on
> final execution. If you cancel before the milestone is hit, that portion is
> refunded. In ND/PA/NJ, the fee is deposited to operating with your informed consent
> and the engagement letter explains termination terms in detail. Either way, you're
> not on the hook for work that hasn't been delivered.

## Pricing escalation rules

The closer can quote up to floor with judgment. Below floor or any non-schedule price
requires Garrison.

| Situation | Closer authority | Escalate to Garrison |
|---|---|---|
| Quote at standard | YES | n/a |
| Quote at floor (if intake supports it — referral source, multi-engagement bundle, fixed budget client stated) | YES | n/a |
| Below floor request | NO | YES — flag `NEGOTIATION_ESCALATE` |
| Custom scope not in schedule | NO | YES — flag `FEE_LOOKUP_MISS` |
| Multi-matter engagement >$10K | YES (apply tier discount) | Notify, don't block |
| Payment plan request | NO | YES |
| Refund request | NO | YES — Garrison reviews |
| Free / pro bono request | NO | YES — Garrison decides |

## How the closer states the fee

Once package is decided, the fee delivery is one sentence:

> "Based on what you've described, the right package is [Comprehensive EP Married] at a
> flat fee of $3,500."

Then close — don't justify, don't itemize unless asked. The package name + the number
+ "flat fee" carries the certainty.

If the client asks "what does that include?" — THEN itemize. Not before.
